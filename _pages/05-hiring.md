Thank you for your inquiry about Tulane Computer Science and working in my lab. I get many of these emails so sorry for the impersonal response here but there are several steps every potential student must take and a timeline that admissions are handled on.

In general, I am always looking for outstanding and motivated students to work on projects broadly related to AI, specifically in the areas of multi-agent systems, social choice, preference reasoning, and reinforcement learning with applications to recommendation systems, natural language processing, and other user focused systems. I am really interested in how these systems are related to social impact and the broad ethics around technology and society. More information on my research is available on my webpage https://www.nickmattei.net/ and most recent publications on Google Scholar https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=bUAYDkIAAAAJ&hl=en.

As a first step, you must apply to the department, as all admissions decisions are made there first. Please follow the process outlined on the webpage: https://sse.tulane.edu/ms-and-phd-programs. Note that there is no application fee associated with this process. Any questions related to admissions, timelines, or official acceptance to the program should be sent to Dr. Ramgopal Mettu at rmettu@tulane.edu.

If you want to work with me, please specifically mention that in your letter / application packet and I will see it and will be in touch if the admissions committee makes a positive recommendation. I do not have any control nor can I answer any questions about how the process is going etc. as all these decisions are made by Dr. Mettu.

Note that I am generally looking for students focused on or interested in the specific AI areas above so please make sure your interests align with mine if you are listing me on your application. A complete list of professors at Tulane is available https://sse.tulane.edu/cs/ms-and-phd-programs and if you are a better match for the research of others please contact them directly.
